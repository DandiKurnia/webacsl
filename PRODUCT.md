# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Prospective and current Universitas Gunadarma students who land on the ACSL site to understand what the lab is and what it does. Most arrive on a phone, often between classes, with little prior context — they're deciding whether ACSL is worth their attention and, eventually, whether to join as an assistant (aslab). Secondary visitors: faculty, university peers, and alumni who use the site as a public face of the lab's work.

## Product Purpose

ACSL (Advanced Computing and Systems Laboratory) is the research and practicum lab of the Informatics/Computer program at Universitas Gunadarma, spanning three campus regions — Depok, Kalimalang, and Karawaci — and four practicum subjects (JKD, JKL, Komputasi Bergerak, FPGA).

The landing page exists to **showcase the lab**: to present ACSL as a credible, active, modern research community and leave visitors with a strong, specific impression of its facilities, people, and work. Success is a visitor who leaves understanding what ACSL is, remembering how it felt, and knowing where to go next (labs, team, joining).

## Brand Personality

Energetic and modern. Forward-looking and active, never stuffy or institutional. The voice is confident and student-driven — Indonesian-first copy, plain and direct, with engineering-grade precision in the details. Three words: **active, precise, current.**

Emotionally, a visitor should feel momentum and competence: this is a lab that does real things, run by people who care about craft. Energy comes from pace, contrast, and purposeful motion — not from hype, noise, or buzzwords.

## Anti-references

No hard anti-reference was specified. The working guard is the universal one: it must not read as a generic corporate/SaaS template or a dated campus portal. Keep it specifically ACSL — carried by the committed brand system, real imagery, and concrete copy — not anonymous and not interchangeable with any other lab or company.

## Design Principles

- **One system, every surface.** The hero established a committed visual language (brand blue, yellow accent, ink/body ramp, mono dot-kickers, fluid `clamp()` headings, radial backdrops, hairline borders). Every section earns trust by speaking it consistently. Consistency of voice is the brand.
- **Showcase, don't just list.** The goal is impression, not inventory. Lead with the lab's people, places, and work as concrete proof; avoid uniform card grids that flatten everything into a catalog.
- **Energy through restraint.** Modern and active, but motion and color are purposeful, never decorative. Pace and contrast carry the energy; hype does not.
- **Place and people are the evidence.** Three regions and a real team are the lab's credibility — give them weight and real imagery rather than abstract claims.
- **Mobile is the primary canvas.** Most visitors are on a phone; every section is designed and tested at small viewports first.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Target WCAG 2.1 AA. Body text holds ≥4.5:1 contrast (large text ≥3:1) against its surface — verified, not eyeballed. Every animation has a `prefers-reduced-motion` fallback. Interactive elements carry visible `focus-visible` indicators and meet 44×44px touch targets. Color is never the sole carrier of meaning. Imagery uses descriptive, voice-consistent alt text.
